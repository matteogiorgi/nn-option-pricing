\section{Conclusions and Next Steps}

The first implementation confirms that a feed-forward neural network can approximate the Black-Scholes pricing function for European call options with high accuracy in a controlled synthetic environment.
The project is therefore consistent with the initial research question and provides a solid basis for further experiments.

The next steps are:

\begin{itemize}
    \item increase the size of the synthetic dataset;
    \item run longer training experiments and compare different architectures;
    \item study the error in specific regions of the parameter space, for instance deep in-the-money or out-of-the-money options;
    \item compare evaluation times across the analytical formula, Monte Carlo simulation, and the neural network;
    \item improve the theoretical discussion of the limitations of a pricing surrogate trained on synthetic data.
\end{itemize}

As future developments, one could consider stochastic volatility models, more complex options, or more advanced neural methods.
These extensions are outside the scope of the first version of the project, but they represent natural directions for generalizing the approach.
