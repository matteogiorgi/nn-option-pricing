\chapter{Methodology}

\section{Synthetic Dataset}

The dataset is generated by randomly sampling option and market parameters.
Each observation contains:

\begin{equation}
    x = (S_0, K, T, r, \sigma),
\end{equation}

and the target is:

\begin{equation}
    y = C_{BS}(S_0, K, T, r, \sigma).
\end{equation}

The initial parameter ranges are:

\begin{table}[H]
    \centering
    \begin{tabular}{lll}
        \toprule
        Variable & Description & Range \\
        \midrule
        $S_0$ & initial underlying price & $[50, 150]$ \\
        $K$ & strike price & $[50, 150]$ \\
        $T$ & maturity in years & $[0.1, 2]$ \\
        $r$ & risk-free rate & $[0, 0.05]$ \\
        $\sigma$ & volatility & $[0.1, 0.6]$ \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
    \caption{Parameter ranges used to generate the synthetic dataset.}
\end{table}

Using synthetic data makes the problem controlled and allows the neural network error to be evaluated directly against the exact target function.

\section{Neural Network}

The model is a feed-forward neural network for regression.
This choice is motivated by the fact that sufficiently large feed-forward networks are universal function approximators under general assumptions \cite{cybenko1989approximation,hornik1989multilayer}.
The initial architecture is:

\begin{equation}
    5 \rightarrow 64 \rightarrow 64 \rightarrow 32 \rightarrow 1,
\end{equation}

with ReLU activation functions in the hidden layers.
The network approximates the function:

\begin{equation}
    f_\theta(S_0, K, T, r, \sigma) \approx C_{BS}.
\end{equation}

The optimized loss is the mean squared error:

\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{L}(\theta) =
    \frac{1}{n}
    \sum_{i=1}^{n}
    \left(f_\theta(x_i) - y_i\right)^2.
\end{equation}

\section{Training and Validation}

The data are split into training, validation, and test sets.
Inputs are standardized using \texttt{StandardScaler}; the target is also scaled during training to improve numerical stability and convergence.
The network is trained with Adam, early stopping, and a fixed random seed for reproducibility.

\section{Metrics}

The main metrics are:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Mean Absolute Error (MAE);
    \item Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE);
    \item coefficient of determination $R^2$;
    \item MAPE computed only on prices greater than 1, to avoid instability when the theoretical option price is close to zero.
\end{itemize}
