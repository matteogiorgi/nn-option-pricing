\section{Preliminary Results}

In the first intermediate experiment, the neural network obtained the following metrics on the test set:

\begin{table}[H]
    \centering
    \begin{tabular}{lr}
        \toprule
        Metric & Value \\
        \midrule
        MAE & 0.6952 \\
        RMSE & 0.9114 \\
        $R^2$ & 0.9985 \\
        MAPE, prices $> 1$ & 10.0137\% \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
    \caption{Preliminary neural network metrics against Black-Scholes prices.}
\end{table}

The high $R^2$ value suggests that the neural network can already approximate the Black-Scholes pricing function very accurately.
The average absolute errors remain limited relative to the overall range of option prices.
MAPE is more delicate to interpret, because the prices of far out-of-the-money options may be close to zero; for this reason, it is computed only on prices greater than 1.

    \centering
    \begin{tabular}{lr}
        \toprule

        \midrule

        \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
    \caption{Preliminary Monte Carlo metrics against Black-Scholes prices.}

The comparison with Monte Carlo is useful because it highlights the difference between a simulation-based numerical method and a supervised model that, once trained, can produce predictions very quickly.
